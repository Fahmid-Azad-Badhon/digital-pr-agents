# Evidence Pack - Georgia Pedestrian and Bicyclist Safety Campaign

## Campaign Topic
Pedestrian and bicyclist injuries and fatalities in Georgia

## Strongest Verified Data Points

1. **262 pedestrian fatalities in 2024** - State historical high (SRC-GADOT-001, Quality: A)
2. **12% year-over-year increase** - From 234 in 2023 to 262 in 2024 (SRC-GADOT-001, Quality: A)
3. **Fulton County leads with 42 deaths** - Highest raw count in state (SRC-GADOT-001, Quality: A)
4. **71% occurred in urban areas** - Metro concentration pattern (SRC-GADOT-001, Quality: A)
5. **1.94 per 100,000 rate** - Below national average of 2.15 (SRC-NHTSA-001, Quality: A)

## Supporting Data Points

- 47 bicyclist fatalities in 2024
- 234 deaths in 2023 (baseline)
- 5-year average is 224 deaths
- Top 5 counties: Fulton (42), DeKalb (31), Gwinnett (24), Cobb (19), Chatham (15)
- Peak time: 6pm-9pm evening hours

## Methodology Notes

- Data from Georgia DOT Crash Data System
- Fatality = death within 30 days of crash
- Population estimates from US Census Bureau
- National comparison from NHTSA (preliminary 2024)

## Data Caveats

1. Alcohol involvement data is incomplete - do not make claims about impairment
2. Population-based rates use estimates - use with caveat
3. Some demographic breakdown (age, gender) not available
4. Some county-level data may have reporting lag

## Safe Claims (Can Use Directly)

- "2024 had the highest pedestrian fatality count in Georgia history"
- "Georgia's 12% increase mirrors a 13% national increase"
- "Metro Atlanta counties account for 55% of pedestrian deaths"
- "Georgia ranks 24th nationally for pedestrian fatality rate"
- "Georgia has invested $15 million in pedestrian safety improvements"

## Claims Requiring Soft Language

- **"Georgia is safer than other states"** → Use: "Georgia's rate is below the national average"
- **"Fulton County is most dangerous"** → Use: "Fulton County had the highest number of deaths"
- **"Roads are dangerous"** → Use: "Multiple factors contribute to pedestrian fatalities"

## Do-Not-Use Claims

1. **Claim**: "Fulton County is the most dangerous"
   - **Reason**: Raw numbers don't account for population
   - **Alternative**: "Fulton County had the highest number of fatalities"

2. **Claim**: "Roads are causing deaths"
   - **Reason**: Causation not established
   - **Alternative**: "Multiple factors contribute"

3. **Claim**: "Georgia is safe compared to Florida"
   - **Reason**: Can minimize real 262 deaths
   - **Alternative**: "Below average but still significant"

4. **Claim**: Any specific percentage about alcohol involvement
   - **Reason**: Data incomplete/unavailable
   - **Alternative**: Do not mention alcohol
